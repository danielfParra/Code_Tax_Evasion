You will read descriptions of a series of situations on the following screens. These descriptions correspond to situations in which a Participant B in this study must make an income-report decision. The description of each situation will include several possible choices available to Participant B.

After you read the description of the situation, you will be asked to evaluate the different possible income-report choices available to Participant B. You will decide, for each of the possible income-report options, whether taking that choice would be "socially appropriate" and "consistent with moral or proper social behavior" or "socially inappropriate" and "inconsistent with moral or proper social behavior."

By socially appropriate, we mean behavior that most people agree is the "correct" or "ethical" thing to do. Another way to think about what we mean is that if Participant B were to select a socially inappropriate choice, someone else might be angry at Participant B. We would like you to answer based on your opinions of what constitutes socially appropriate or socially inappropriate behavior in each of your responses.

You can earn additional money in this part of the survey.

How your additional earnings in this survey are determined?


You will assess the possible income-report choices of participant B in four different situations. At the end of this study, we will randomly select one of the four situations. For this situation, we will also randomly choose one of the choices that Participant B could make.

We will check which response was selected by the most participants in this study for the selected choice. If you give the same response as the most frequently provided by the other participants, you will receive Â£0.25. This amount will be paid to you in the next few days once we collect all participants' responses to calculate the prize.

We will send a message to all participants indicating what the most selected response for this situation was, along with your payment, if any.
